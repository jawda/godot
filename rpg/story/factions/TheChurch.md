# The Church of the Ascending Dawn

## Overview

The Church of the Ascending Dawn is the dominant religious institution across the region, present in every town large enough to have a market square. It preaches a doctrine of light over darkness, ascension over stagnation, the triumph of a singular God over the old pagan powers that once held the land. Its cathedrals are tall and its reach is long. Most people are born, married, and buried under its roof without ever questioning it.

It has held power for seven hundred years.

It is built on a lie.

---

## The Secret

Seven centuries ago, the founders of the Church did not destroy the ancient power sleeping beneath the Mirewald. They found it, studied it, and made a decision that has defined every generation since: they learned to *drain* it.

The old god — the entity the Covenant calls the Sleeping Root and the Church calls the Unnamed Abomination — radiates a slow, vast current of power through the ley lines beneath the land. The Church's founders devised a ritual architecture: churches built on specific sites, bell towers tuned to specific frequencies, liturgical calendars aligned with specific celestial events. Together, the Church functions as an enormous, centuries-long siphon.

Every miracle the Church has ever performed — every healing, every answered prayer, every divine light in the darkness — has been drawn from the sleeping god's unconscious dreaming.

Their God is real only because they stole the power to make him real.

This is what Serafin found in the texts beneath the Mirewald. This is what they cannot allow to be known.

---

## Why the Darkness is Growing

The ancient entity is waking up.

After seven centuries of slow drainage, something has shifted. The Church's scholars believe the entity's dreams have grown lighter — that it is becoming aware of what has been done to it. As it stirs, the siphon becomes unstable. Power that once flowed quietly now surges and ebbs unpredictably. The darkness over Duskholm is not the Covenant's doing alone — it is the entity beginning to *pull back*, reclaiming what was taken.

The Church knows this. They have known for decades that the waking was coming. Their response has been to tighten their grip — burn more heretics, seal more texts, destroy any site that might accelerate the awakening. Ashveil was not only Serafin's operation.

The Church ordered it first. Serafin simply got there before their own inquisitors did, for different reasons.

---

## Structure & Hierarchy

### The Curia — The Inner Circle
A council of seven senior clerics who know the full truth of the Church's founding. They are not mustache-twirling villains — most are elderly scholars who inherited a secret they did not choose and have spent their lives rationalizing why the lie is necessary. They believe, genuinely, that if the truth came out, civilization would collapse. They are probably right.

### The Inquisition
The enforcement arm. Inquisitors like Serafin once was — intelligent, trained, loyal. Most do not know the full truth. They are told they are fighting genuine darkness. Many of them are brave, principled people doing terrible things for reasons they believe are good.

### The Parish Clergy
Priests, healers, confessors. Almost entirely ignorant of the deeper truth. They pray, they serve their communities, they believe completely. Some of the most decent people in the story will be parish priests.

### The Order of the Sealed Eye
A secretive internal order within the Church dedicated specifically to monitoring and suppressing knowledge of the old religion. They are the ones who maintain the siphon architecture — maintaining the bell tower tunings, resetting the liturgical alignments, quietly removing anyone who starts asking the wrong questions. They wear no special insignia. They look like ordinary clergy.

---

## Presence in Duskholm

The Church's representative in Duskholm is **Canon Aldric Strell** — a mid-ranking cleric sent three weeks before the darkness began, ostensibly to minister to the town during a period of "spiritual unease." He arrived with two acolytes and too many locked trunks.

Strell is a member of the Order of the Sealed Eye. He knows something is wrong with the siphon in this region. He does not know Serafin is here. He does not know the player exists. He is working to re-stabilize the siphon before the Curia sends a full Inquisition team and starts asking questions he cannot answer.

He is not evil. He is frightened and competent and in over his head, and he will do whatever he believes is necessary to contain the situation quietly.

---

## Relationship to the Player

The Church's relationship to the player depends on class and backstory, but several threads are consistent:

- **They know about Ashveil.** Church records list it as a "heresy suppression" operation — their operation, which was pre-empted by a rogue agent (Serafin). They have an outstanding file on the Ashveil survivor.
- **Strell will approach the player** once he realizes they are not an ordinary traveller. He will be cautious, offering help and information in exchange for cooperation. He will not immediately lie — he will simply withhold.
- **The Church can be a temporary ally** against the Covenant. Stopping Serafin's ritual serves both the player's goals and the Church's interests. This alliance will feel deeply uncomfortable once the player understands what the Church is protecting.
- **Late game:** the player may have to choose between completing Serafin's ritual (which destroys the Church's lie but may unleash something uncontrollable) and helping the Church reseal the entity (which preserves a corrupt order but maintains stability).

---

## Moral Position

The Church is the most morally complex faction in the story. They are not simply evil:

- The stability they have maintained has kept wars smaller, famines shorter, communities intact
- Many of their clergy are genuinely good people
- Their lie has, arguably, worked for seven hundred years
- The alternative — the entity fully waking — is genuinely unknown and possibly catastrophic

But they have burned innocents, suppressed truth, and built an entire civilization's faith on stolen power. And they will do it again without hesitation.

The question the player must eventually answer is: *is a lie that works better than a truth that might not?*
