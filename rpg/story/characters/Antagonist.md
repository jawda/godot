# Antagonist — Serafin Voss

## Overview

Serafin Voss was once the most trusted inquisitor of the Church — the man they sent when a village had gone wrong, when heresy needed to be quietly buried. He was brilliant, ruthless, and faithful. He believed, completely, that the Church was the last wall between humanity and the dark.

Then he read the texts beneath the Mirewald.

He didn't fall. He didn't break. He simply understood something he could never unlearn: the Church was not the wall. It was the cage. The ancient power sleeping in the Mirewald was not a demon to be sealed. It was the original god of the land — the one the Church had buried under stone and doctrine seven centuries ago — and it was waking up.

Serafin did not abandon his faith. He transferred it.

He now leads the Ashen Covenant, a sect that believes the world must be returned to darkness before it can be reborn into light. The Hollow Sun is not a symbol of despair — to them, it is a promise. The sun does not die when it sets. It rises again. But first, it must fully descend.

Duskholm is the last site. The ritual is almost complete.

---

## The Destruction of Ashveil

Seven years ago, Serafin led the burning of Ashveil. Not out of cruelty — out of necessity. Ashveil sat directly above the first of three sacred convergence points, ancient nodes of power tied to the Mirewald's sleeping god. The village had to be cleared. The ground had to be consecrated in the old way.

He gave the order without hesitation. He still believes it was right.

What he did not anticipate was a survivor.

The player should not have lived. No one was supposed to live. When Serafin learned of the survivor in the weeks after, he made a decision that has defined everything since: he chose not to hunt them down. Something in the augury told him the survivor was not an accident. They were a variable. One he might need.

He has been watching the player ever since. The note under the door in Duskholm was not a threat. It was an invitation — and the first honest thing he has said to anyone in years.

---

## Motivation

Serafin is not trying to end the world. He is trying to end *this* world — the one built on suppression, fear, and a lie — so that something true can replace it.

He has watched the Church burn innocents to protect its secrets. He has read what was buried. He has seen the Mirewald's god stir in response to the rituals, felt its presence — vast, patient, without malice. He believes this entity is not evil. It is simply old. Older than sin. Older than the Church's god.

He believes:
- The sun must fully set (the ritual completed) before any new light can rise
- The thirty days of darkness over Duskholm is not a curse — it is a gestation
- What is born when the ritual completes will be incorruptible, because it predates corruption

He is calm. He is certain. He does not want to fight the player. He wants them to understand.

---

## Why He Is Frightening

Serafin is not wrong about everything. The Church *is* hiding something. The ancient texts *are* real. The entity in the Mirewald *is* not mindlessly evil.

But he has committed atrocities in service of a truth he only partially understands. He destroyed Ashveil — killed hundreds of people — based on a reading of ancient texts written by people who may have been just as wrong as the Church he despises. He has never truly questioned whether the entity he is awakening *wants* what he believes it wants, or whether it is simply hungry.

He is a true believer. And true believers are the most dangerous kind of person.

---

## Relationship to the Player

Serafin feels something close to guilt about Ashveil — not guilt enough to undo his conviction, but enough that he has never been able to fully treat the player as expendable. This is his only real weakness.

He will attempt to recruit the player before he resorts to conflict. He will tell them the truth about Ashveil — his version of it, which is factually accurate but morally catastrophic. He will offer them a place in what comes after.

If the player refuses and moves to stop him, he will be genuinely sorrowful. And then he will try to kill them.

---

## Appearance

Late fifties. Tall, lean, with the posture of a man who spent decades in formal robes. His Church vestments have been stripped of their insignia and dyed black. He wears the Hollow Sun as a brand on the inside of his left wrist — not tattooed, *burned*. His voice is quiet. He does not raise it. He has learned that the quieter you speak, the harder people listen.
