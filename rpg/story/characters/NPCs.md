# NPCs

## Overview

Duskholm's remaining population is small enough that the player will encounter most key characters multiple times. NPCs should feel like they have lives beyond their function — they reference each other, have opinions about the darkness, and change subtly as the story progresses.

---

## Maret
**Role:** Innkeeper, Tallow Inn
**Age:** 48
**Romance:** No

### Appearance
Stocky and unhurried, with greying auburn hair she keeps in a practical braid. Hands roughened from decades of work. Always has an apron on. The kind of face that has laughed a lot and worried a lot in roughly equal measure, and shows both.

### Personality
Direct, warm, unsentimental. She does not coddle people but she also does not turn them away. She has seen enough travellers pass through the Tallow Inn to read a person quickly and she reads the player correctly from the first conversation — not as an ordinary traveller, and not as a threat. Something else. She is not sure what yet.

She is the emotional anchor of Duskholm. When people do not know what to do, they come to the inn and sit near the fire and let Maret refill their cup and do not ask for advice but somehow feel better anyway.

### What She Knows
More than she lets on. She has lived in Duskholm for forty-eight years and she has been watching Canon Strell's locked trunks and odd hours since he arrived. She knows about the tunnel entrance in the Abandoned Quarter — her late husband found it twenty years ago and they agreed never to speak of it. She does not know about the Covenant specifically, but she knows that something has been using the north end of town in ways that aren't natural.

### What She Doesn't Know
The Church's secret. The entity. Anything about Ashveil or the player's history.

### Arc
Maret starts as an information source and becomes something more — one of the few people in Duskholm who, once she decides to trust the player, trusts them completely. In the late game she quietly organizes the remaining townsfolk without making speeches about it, which turns out to be more effective.

### Key Dialogue Beats
- First meeting: sizes the player up, gives them the basics of the town's situation without sentimentality
- After the player finds first Covenant evidence: "I wondered when someone would start asking those questions"
- Late game: reveals the tunnel, reveals what she knows about Strell — she has been keeping it until someone seemed worth telling

## Brother Edvin
**Role:** Apothecary
**Age:** 67
**Romance:** No

### Appearance
Thin and slightly stooped, with wispy white hair and very clean hands despite working with herbs and compounds all day. Wears his old Church healer's robes with the insignia unpicked — not in protest, just because the fabric is good and he is practical. Moves slowly but precisely.

### Personality
Gentle, methodical, and quietly melancholy in the way of someone who has seen too much suffering and made peace with not being able to fix all of it. He still believes in the Church's God — or rather, he still believes in the goodness he has seen people do in that God's name, and he has learned to separate the institution from the faith. He is not naive. He is just choosing what to hold onto.

He is endlessly patient with the player and will explain everything he knows without being asked twice.

### What He Knows
The botanical texts in his possession describe Mirewald plants in terms suggesting pre-Church medicinal and ritual use — he has been reading them during the darkness and growing increasingly uneasy. He has treated the "dimming" symptoms in townsfolk and has a theory: the energy that has always flowed quietly through Duskholm is disrupted, and people who are sensitive to it are being affected. He does not have the framework to understand what this means but his observations are accurate.

He also knew Canon Strell by reputation, years ago. A capable man. The fact that Strell is here, now, concerns him in ways he cannot quite articulate.

### What He Doesn't Know
The siphon. The Church's real secret. What the "dimming" actually is.

### Arc
Edvin's arc is about the gap between faith and institution. As the player reveals the Church's truth to him — in stages, gently or bluntly depending on player choice — he has to decide what remains of his belief when the foundation turns out to be stolen. His final position is his own and should feel earned rather than prescribed.

### Quest Hooks
- His botanical texts are a lore item that unlocks context about the Mirewald's history
- The "dimming" symptoms quest — he asks the player to investigate, leads to ley line discovery
- Can identify materials from deep Mirewald that other characters cannot

## Corren
**Role:** Blacksmith
**Age:** 24
**Romance:** Yes (female option)

### Appearance
Lean and wiry with functional muscle built from years at the forge. Medium height, dark skin, natural hair kept tied back under a leather wrap while she works. Burns and small scars on her forearms that she has never bothered to treat properly. Usually has soot somewhere on her face. Brown eyes that assess things — people included — with the same calm efficiency she'd use to judge a piece of metalwork.

### Personality
Blunt, competent, self-reliant to a fault. She does not perform warmth but it exists — you can see it in the reinforced shutters she's installed for neighbours who couldn't pay her, in the way she knows everyone's name and what they need without making a thing of it. She has a dry sense of humour that surfaces unexpectedly. She laughs with her whole face.

She does not suffer fools. She has no patience for people who say things they don't mean or ask questions they already know the answer to. She will tell the player exactly what she thinks of their decisions, including the bad ones, especially the bad ones.

### What She Knows
She has been noticing things — the way certain buildings in the Abandoned Quarter have been interfered with, the odd deliveries Canon Strell received in the first week. She does not have theories, just observations she has been filing away with no one to tell them to. The player is the first person who has seemed worth telling.

She also knows every structural weakness in Duskholm's walls, every building with a good defensive position, every person in town she would or wouldn't trust in a crisis. This knowledge becomes practically useful in the late game.

### What She Doesn't Know
Anything about the Covenant, the Church's secret, or the entity. She is starting from scratch with the player.

### Romance Arc
Corren does not respond to flattery or gestures. She responds to the player showing up and doing things without making it about themselves. The arc progresses through shared tasks — she asks for help with something practical, the player delivers, she notices. It takes a long time before she says anything that could be called personal. The first time she does — something small, honest, not dressed up — it lands harder than anything more theatrical would.

A late-stage moment: she is working the forge at night, alone, and the player finds her there. She doesn't stop working. They talk. It is the first conversation where she does not have a tool in her hand as armour.

### Quest Hooks
- Weapons and armour shop from the start
- Mid-game: asks the player to retrieve materials from the Mirewald she can't get herself
- Late game: becomes a key figure in town defence planning

## Warden Osric
**Role:** Town Guard Captain
**Age:** 51
**Romance:** No

### Appearance
Broad and weathered, with a soldier's posture he has never lost. Close-cropped grey hair, a short beard going the same direction. The kind of man who looks like he was born in armour. He is not wearing armour right now — just his warden's coat — and this small fact communicates more about his current state than anything he says.

### Personality
Methodical, fair, deeply uncomfortable with uncertainty. He is good at problems with parameters — a brawl, a theft, a fire. The current situation has no parameters he can hold onto and it is slowly grinding him down. He is not giving up. He is trying to maintain structure in a situation where structure is the only thing he knows how to offer.

He will be suspicious of the player initially and will not pretend otherwise. He will warm slowly and respect the player's competence before he respects them personally.

### What He Knows
He sealed the gates on day one of the darkness — his call, he stands by it. He has been receiving reports of creature sightings near the north wall for two weeks and has been officially attributing them to "livestock" to prevent panic. He is no longer convinced that is true. He is aware that Canon Strell is not simply a pastoral minister but does not know what else he is. He has a list of eleven people who have gone missing in Duskholm in the past thirty days — not fled through the postern gate, actually missing — that he has told no one about.

### What He Doesn't Know
Anything about the Covenant's presence inside town. The missing persons list and the Covenant tunnel system are connected and he has not made that link yet.

### Arc
Osric's arc is about the limits of institutional authority. He eventually has to choose between following the protocol he believes in and responding to a reality his protocol cannot contain. When he makes that choice — and he makes it the right way — he is one of the most quietly effective people in the late game.

### Quest Hooks
- The missing persons list is a major mid-game quest trigger
- He can grant the player access to guard patrol records, which contain clues
- Late game: can mobilise the town guard if trust is fully established

## Pell
**Role:** Gravedigger
**Age:** 23
**Romance:** No

### Appearance
Thin and pale in a way that looks deliberate but isn't. Lank dark hair that falls across his face and that he pushes aside constantly without registering he's doing it. Bright, slightly too-attentive eyes. Always has dirt under his fingernails. Smiles easily and at slightly unexpected moments.

### Personality
Cheerful in a way that other people find unnerving, which genuinely puzzles him. He likes his work — not in a sinister way, in the way that someone likes a job that is quiet and necessary and theirs. He talks to the dead sometimes. He finds them better listeners than most of the living.

He is not naive or slow — he notices things acutely and processes them in his own rhythm, which is slower than most people's but arrives at accurate conclusions. He has been keeping a detailed mental log of everything strange he has observed in the graveyard and the Abandoned Quarter because it is interesting, not because he thinks it is important, and he has no idea how important it actually is.

### What He Knows
Three graves have been disturbed from the inside over the past two weeks. He has found chalk markings near the north cemetery wall that match markings he has seen on doors in the Abandoned Quarter. He found something in one of the disturbed graves that he reburied because he did not know what else to do with it — a carved stone disc bearing the Hollow Sun symbol.

### What He Doesn't Know
What any of it means. He has been waiting for someone to ask.

### Arc
Pell is a minor character with disproportionate lore significance. The carved disc he reburied is a significant item. His observations about the disturbed graves lead directly to a Covenant subplot. He never becomes a main quest figure but the player who talks to him will understand the story better.

### Quest Hooks
- The disturbed graves quest — leads to Covenant consecration ritual evidence
- The carved stone disc — significant item pickup
- He has observed Covenant members moving through the Abandoned Quarter at night and can describe them

## Cael
**Role:** Cartographer, Glasshouse regular
**Age:** 26
**Romance:** Yes (male option)

### Appearance
Slight and dark-haired, with the kind of face that looks younger than it is until he is thinking hard about something, which is most of the time. Always has ink on his fingers — sometimes his cheek. Dresses practically but slightly wrong for the weather, as someone does when they move around a lot and pack without paying attention. Has a habit of tilting his head when he listens to people, as if he's mapping them.

### Personality
Genuinely curious about everything in a way that is not performative — he is not asking questions to seem interested, he is asking them because the answer will bother him until he has it. He is warm and quick and slightly awkward in a way he is aware of and has mostly made peace with. He finds things funny often, including things that are not quite funny, which is either charming or irritating depending on the situation.

He is braver than he looks and less prepared for that fact than he should be. He keeps doing things that scare him because the alternative is not knowing, and not knowing is worse.

### What He Knows
Six notebooks worth of observations — creature movements, bell tower behaviour, Abandoned Quarter patterns, the geometry of the Hollow Sun markings he found on three buildings before he understood what they were. He does not have the context to interpret most of it. He has an extremely accurate map of Duskholm including several features that are not on any official record, including a second entrance to the tunnel system he found by accident and has not gone into.

### What He Doesn't Know
Almost everything about the story. He is the most informationally useful character for the *player's* investigation despite knowing the least about the *actual* situation — because he has been documenting evidence without the bias of knowing what it is.

### Romance Arc
Cael is interested in the player almost immediately and does a middling job of concealing it. He does not pursue it — he is genuinely unsure whether what he feels is real or just the intensity of a frightening situation making everything feel more significant. His arc is about working out the difference.

He says the wrong thing at the right moment with some regularity — honest when deflection would be kinder, quiet when the player needs to hear something. A mid-game moment: he gives the player a page from one of his notebooks — not the information on it, just the page. The information is a pretext. He wanted to give them something of his.

### Quest Hooks
- His notebooks are a major optional lore source
- The second tunnel entrance he found can bypass a mid-game obstacle
- Late game: his maps become essential for navigating the Mirewald's deeper sections

## Sera
**Role:** Glasshouse keeper
**Age:** 63
**Romance:** No

### Appearance
Short and broad, with white hair worn loose and a face full of deep lines that all go the right direction — the kind of face that has spent a lot of time smiling and squinting in firelight. Strong hands, slow movements, the particular unhurriedness of someone who decided decades ago that rushing is for other people.

### Personality
She is the warmest person in Duskholm. Not in a cloying way — in a way that feels like a fire you did not know you needed until you were standing next to it. She does not ask questions about where you came from or what you have done. She makes tea and she listens and she occasionally says something that lands with uncomfortable accuracy.

She is not naive. She has simply chosen, after a long life, to put her energy into making things alive rather than defending against things that are dying. She is aware that this is a choice, not an inevitability, and she makes it every morning.

### What She Knows
Very little about the specific events of the story. She knows something fundamental has changed in the land's character — she has kept plants alive through hard winters and droughts and she can feel that this is different, deeper. She has lived in Duskholm long enough to remember when the forest was merely unpleasant rather than actively wrong.

She knew Edvin vaguely, decades ago, when he was still a Church healer passing through. She has a long memory for people.

### Arc
Sera is a constant — the Glasshouse does not change regardless of what happens in the main story, and neither does she. She is available to the player throughout the game as a place to breathe. In the late game, if Duskholm is threatened, her response is to put more lights in the Glasshouse windows. This is her answer to everything and it is not wrong.

## Nessa
**Role:** Squatter, Abandoned Quarter
**Age:** 22
**Romance:** Yes (female option)

### Appearance
Small and sharp-featured, with dark eyes that clock everything in a room before she has finished entering it. Wiry. Keeps her dark hair cut short — practical, not stylistic. Wears mismatched layers scavenged from the houses around her that somehow look intentional. Moves quietly without consciously trying to.

### Personality
Guarded, observant, and several steps ahead of most conversations before they start. She learned early that trusting people easily is a way of getting hurt, and she has not unlearned it. She is not cold — she can be funny, dry, unexpectedly kind — but these appear in the gaps between defences, not as a default.

She is honest to a fault when she does speak. She has no patience for comfortable lies, which is partly why she left the Church.

### What She Knows
Four years of Church training in what she now understands was the Order of the Sealed Eye — archival suppression, identifying and neutralising unauthorised information about the old religion, the theory (though not full practice) of the siphon architecture. She memorised three documents before they were destroyed, including the one that led her to Duskholm. She can read the Hollow Sun markings, identify Covenant ritual sites, and interpret bell tower architecture in ways no one else in Duskholm can.

She knows Serafin Voss by name and reputation. She does not know he is in Duskholm. When she finds out, she will need time to process it — he was a genuinely good teacher and she has been trying to decide for eight months whether anything he taught her is still true.

### What She Doesn't Know
What Serafin is actually doing in Duskholm. The full extent of the ritual. Anything about the player's connection to Ashveil.

### Romance Arc
Nessa tests people before she trusts them, sometimes without realising she is doing it. She will present the player with small, deniable situations — leaving information where they can find it, putting herself in low-level danger to see if they notice — and observe the results. She does not consciously think of this as romantic. She thinks of it as due diligence.

The arc turns on a single moment of honesty from the player — something they could have concealed but didn't. After that, she stops testing. She does not announce this. She just stops.

A late-stage scene: she shows the player the house she has made habitable in the Abandoned Quarter. The candles she has lit, the small repairs, the book she found in a neighbour's abandoned shelf. It is the first time she has shown anyone where she lives and what she has made of it.

### Quest Hooks
- First encounter: the player finds evidence of someone living in the Abandoned Quarter before they find Nessa
- Her knowledge of Sealed Eye operations unlocks major Church subplot
- Mid-game: she deciphers a Covenant document the player retrieves that no one else can read
- Her reaction to learning Serafin is present is a significant story moment

## Rowan
**Role:** Covenant member, Tallow Inn
**Age:** 32
**Romance:** Yes (male option)

### Appearance
Broad-shouldered and quietly built, with a face that defaults to neutral in a way that takes effort. Dark hair going slightly grey at the temples earlier than it should — stress, probably. A small scar at the corner of his mouth from something he does not explain. He looks like someone who has been carrying something heavy for a long time and has learned not to show the weight.

### Personality
Measured, honest, and methodical in the way of someone who spent a long time being certain and is no longer. He does not perform emotion but he does not suppress it — he simply processes it before it arrives in his face, which means conversations with him have a slight delay to them, like he is translating. He says what he means without decoration. He is wrong about things sometimes and he acknowledges it without self-flagellation, which is rarer than it sounds.

He has a dry wit that surfaces in moments of stress, which is a survival mechanism he is only partially aware of.

### What He Knows
Three years of Covenant operations. The location and purpose of every ritual site in the Mirewald. The full text of the doctrine Serafin has built the Covenant around. The identities of Covenant members in Duskholm. What happens to people who volunteer for deep consecration.

He joined because the Church destroyed his village — a real thing that really happened and that he was right to be angry about. He has been trying to work out when the anger stopped being the point.

### What He Doesn't Know
Who the player is or why they are in Duskholm. Whether his doubts are conscience or cowardice. Whether there is a version of stopping this that doesn't require him to betray people who trusted him.

### Romance Arc
Rowan does not flirt. He has no apparatus for it. He is drawn to the player because they are the first person in a long time who he can be honest with about his actual position — not performing certainty for the Covenant, not performing innocence for Duskholm. The honesty itself is the beginning of it.

His arc is inseparable from his moral arc. He cannot fully open to the player while he is still unresolved about what he is doing, and the player helping him find that resolution — or failing to — determines the shape of the relationship.

A mid-game moment: he tells the player about his village. Not what happened to him after it, not what it led to — just what it was. What it looked like on an ordinary morning. It is the first thing he has said that is entirely without agenda. He looks surprised by himself afterward.

If the player romances Rowan, Nessa will have opinions. Strong ones. This should be a real conversation that the player has to navigate, not a pass/fail check.

### Quest Hooks
- First contact: slips the player information about a Covenant location when their investigation gets close enough
- His knowledge of ritual sites is essential for mid and late game Mirewald navigation
- He can arrange a meeting with lower-ranking Covenant members who share his doubts
- The Nessa/Rowan tension is a side quest in its own right
- His final choice — whether to face Serafin directly — is a late-game scene tied to both his quest arc and the romance arc

## Canon Aldric Strell
**Role:** Church representative, Chapel of the Ascending Dawn
**Age:** 44
**Romance:** No

### Appearance
Precise and well-maintained in a way that feels deliberate even after weeks in the field. Dark vestments that have been pressed despite circumstances. Close-cropped dark hair with grey beginning at the temples. A face trained to project pastoral calm that occasionally slips to reveal something more calculating beneath.

### Personality
Intelligent, controlled, and genuinely conflicted. He is not a villain who enjoys what he does — he is a man who believes the system he serves is imperfect but necessary, and that maintaining it is a moral act. He will be helpful to the player to the extent that their goals align with his, which is further than the player might expect. He will lie by omission as a first resort and by commission only when he feels he has no alternative.

He respects competence. If the player demonstrates it he will treat them as an equal rather than an asset to be managed, which is his highest form of regard.

### What He Knows
The full truth of the Church's siphon architecture. The entity beneath the Mirewald. The purpose of the bell towers. That the siphon is destabilizing. That Serafin Voss was an Inquisitor who went rogue and that he is almost certainly connected to what is happening in Duskholm — though he does not yet know Serafin is physically present.

### What He Doesn't Know
The player's history. Nessa's presence in town. The full extent of Covenant operations inside the walls.

### Arc
Strell is the player's most complicated ally. He will offer genuine help because he needs the player's help — stopping the Covenant serves the Church's interests as much as anyone's. But his version of stopping it is resealing the entity and returning to the status quo, which the player will eventually understand means preserving the lie.

His final scene — when the player has to decide what to do with him, and with what he represents — should feel like a genuine moral weight rather than a clear answer.

### Quest Hooks
- First meeting: he approaches the player after observing their investigation, offers information in exchange for cooperation
- Mid-game: he reveals (partially) the true purpose of the bell towers
- His locked trunks contain Church operational documents that the player can access through multiple routes
- Late game: his cooperation or non-cooperation significantly affects what options the player has

## Brin
**Role:** Apothecary assistant (missing)
**Age:** 19
**Romance:** No

### Appearance
Small and slightly round-faced with a dusting of freckles and the perpetually ink-stained fingers of someone who takes detailed notes about everything. Usually wore her red-brown hair in two braids. Practical, cheerful-looking. The kind of face that makes people assume she is younger than she is and less capable than she is, both of which annoyed her.

When the player finds her in the Saltmere Vein she is sitting cross-legged in the bioluminescent dark, her hair loose and matted, eyes too wide and too still. She is breathing slowly. She looks peaceful in a way that is not peaceful at all.

### Personality
Eager, organised, and quietly ambitious. She was not content to be an assistant forever — she was learning everything Edvin would teach her with the specific intention of opening her own practice one day, somewhere with a bit more sunlight. She asked questions constantly. She wrote everything down. Edvin found her exhausting and indispensable in roughly equal measure.

She is resourceful enough that she survived three weeks in the cave. She gathered food from what was available, managed the Cave Grippers with light discipline, and kept herself alive. The Deepcap spores did the rest — not hurting her, just softening the edges of everything until staying felt easier than leaving.

### What She Knows
More than she is currently able to access. Her notes from three weeks of cave observation — written in a small notebook she keeps in her coat — contain detailed observations about the ley line glow, the fungi growth patterns, and the Cave Gripper behaviour shift. She recorded everything methodically right up until the spores took hold. The notebook is legible and scientifically valuable. She does not remember writing most of it.

She also overheard a conversation at the apothecary two days before she left — two men she did not recognise, asking Edvin about specific compounds. She did not think much of it at the time. In hindsight, the compounds they asked about are used in Covenant consecration rituals. This is a detail she will surface in a late conversation after she has recovered.

### What She Doesn't Know
That she has been gone three weeks. It feels like less. Much less.

### Arc
Getting Brin out of the cave requires solving the spore exposure puzzle — not violence, not a skill check, but working out that the Cave Gripper disturbance would flush the spore concentration from the deep section if handled correctly, allowing her to come back to herself gradually.

Her recovery takes time. Back in Duskholm she is quieter than she was, which Edvin notices immediately. She is not traumatised in a dramatic way — more like something was reshuffled inside her and she is still working out what is where. She returns to work at the apothecary. She starts keeping a second notebook, separate from her work notes, that she does not explain.

In the late game she surfaces the overheard conversation unprompted, having finally placed its significance. She does not make a large thing of it. She just puts her notebook on the player's table and points at the relevant page.

### Quest Hooks
- Her disappearance is the trigger for the Saltmere Vein quest
- Her cave notebook is a significant lore item
- The overheard conversation is a late-game Covenant lead
- Her recovery gives Edvin a secondary character arc — his guilt, his relief, the conversation about what happened that they both keep postponing
