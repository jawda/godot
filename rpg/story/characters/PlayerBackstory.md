# Player Backstory

## Overview

The player is a survivor of Ashveil — a village that no longer exists.

Seven years ago, Ashveil burned. Not from war, not from accident. It was erased. Every building, every field, every face you knew — gone in a single night. You were the only one who walked out of the ash.

You never found out why.

You've spent the years since then drifting. Taking work where you can find it, moving on before questions catch up with you. You told yourself you were searching for answers. Mostly you were just running.

Then you came to Duskholm. You didn't choose it — something drew you here, a pull you couldn't name. And when you found the note under your door bearing the same symbol carved into the ruins of Ashveil, everything you'd buried came roaring back.

Someone knows what happened that night. Someone has been waiting for you to arrive.

---

## The Symbol

The wax seal bears a mark called **the Hollow Sun** — a circle bisected by a downward line, like a sun setting below the horizon. You first saw it etched into the foundation stones of Ashveil's chapel, uncovered in the rubble the morning after the fire.

It doesn't belong to any church, any noble house, or any guild you've ever encountered. Every time you've asked about it, people either go silent or don't remember the conversation.

---

## Tone & Motivation

The player is not a hero by calling. They are someone who wants answers and has learned — the hard way — that survival requires skill.

**Primary drive:** Find out what destroyed Ashveil and why they were spared.
**Secondary drive:** Understand what the Hollow Sun means and who controls it.
**Hidden fear:** That they weren't spared by accident — that they were *kept* for a purpose.

---

## Class Interpretations

The backstory is designed to be class-agnostic. The trauma of Ashveil shaped the player, but *how* they responded to it defines their class. Below are suggested framings:

### Warrior / Knight
After Ashveil, you hired yourself out as a sword — mercenary work, caravan protection, the occasional dirty job. You got good at not dying. You stopped asking what the job was for and focused on getting it done. The calluses on your hands are the only proof Ashveil ever existed.

### Rogue / Shadowblade
Survival after Ashveil meant invisibility. You moved through towns like smoke, taking what you needed, trusting no one, leaving before dawn. Your skills aren't tricks — they're scars shaped into something useful.

### Mage / Arcanist
The night of the fire, you felt something pass through you — a cold wave, like a hand reaching inside your chest and letting go. Since then, the world has looked different. Thinner. Like a curtain over something vast. You've been teaching yourself to pull back the edges ever since.

### Cleric / Faithbound
The Church found you wandering the road out of Ashveil, barely conscious, covered in ash. They took you in, gave you purpose, gave you God. You've served faithfully. But the Church's God doesn't explain the Hollow Sun — and the closer you get to the truth, the more you wonder if faith is the answer or the blindfold.

### Ranger / Wayfinder
You left the ruins and never stopped walking. The wilds became safer than towns — quieter, more honest. You know forests, roads, the language of tracks and weather. You've been following something for seven years without knowing what it was. Now you're starting to think it was leading you here.
