# Main Quest — The Hollow Sun

## Overview

The main quest is structured in three acts. Each act ends with a revelation that reframes everything the player thought they understood. The central dramatic question is not "who is the villain" — it is "what do you do when everyone is wrong in ways that matter and right in ways that also matter."

The player has three possible endings, none of which is clearly correct.

---

## The Ritual — Background

The Covenant's ritual requires the consecration of three convergence points — ancient nodes where ley lines intersect and the entity's energy concentrates closest to the surface.

| Point | Location | Status at game start |
|---|---|---|
| First | Ashveil | Consecrated 7 years ago. The village was destroyed in the process. |
| Second | The Rootwell, Duskholm | Consecrated 30 days ago. This is what caused the darkness to begin. |
| Third | The Rootbed, Mirewald heart | Not yet consecrated. This is the final step. The ritual completes here. |

The player arrives in Duskholm thirty days after the second consecration. Serafin is waiting for them. He has been waiting for seven years.

---

## Act One — What Happened Here

*The player is trapped in a dark town and does not know why they cannot leave.*

### Key Beats

**1. Arrival**
The player wakes at the Tallow Inn. The note is on the floor. Three words: *"You were summoned."*
The gates will not open. The town is dark. Something moves at the treeline.

**2. First Investigation**
The player begins talking to Duskholm's remaining residents. Maret gives the lay of the land. Osric is suspicious but not hostile. Strell is visible at the chapel and watching.

Early exploration of the Abandoned Quarter reveals Covenant markings the player cannot yet read. First Tier 1 creature encounter at the forest edge at night.

**3. Strell Makes Contact**
Canon Strell approaches the player after observing their investigation for two or three days. He is measured and careful. He offers information — partial, accurate — in exchange for cooperation. He tells the player the Covenant is responsible for the darkness and that their ritual, if completed, will cause irreversible harm. He does not yet say what the ritual is for or what he knows about the entity.

He is genuinely helpful. He is also genuinely managing the player.

**4. The Rootwell Vision**
After resting at the Tallow Inn two or three times, the player is pulled from sleep by a cold blue-white light coming from Rootwell Square. They walk to it without fully deciding to. The Rootwell is glowing from deep below.

What follows is a flashback sequence — five fragments, deliberately vague. A childhood warmth that may be Ashveil. An ancient Hollow Sun carving older than the Covenant. Something like the player's name, heard somewhere other than the ears. The sound of Ashveil's bells, ringing backwards. And finally: the root system beneath the world, lit from within, and a presence so vast that its awareness of the player is like being noticed by the ocean.

The player comes back to themselves kneeling at the cold well. No explanation is offered.

See `story/dialogue/RootwellVision.md` for the full scene script and design notes.

This is also where the player can first confirm (if they have found the right Covenant markings) that the Rootwell was recently used as a ritual site. The second consecration happened here. The darkness started the morning after.

**5. First Meeting with Serafin**
Serafin finds the player. He does not ambush them — he simply appears, in a place the player is alone, and speaks.

He does not lie. He tells the player that he knows who they are, that he has known for seven years, and that the note was his. He does not apologise for Ashveil. He explains it. Calmly. Completely. As if it were a reasonable thing that happened.

He tells the player that the ritual is not destruction — it is correction. He invites them to understand before they decide to oppose him. He leaves without fighting.

This is the end of Act One. The player now knows:
- Serafin destroyed Ashveil and is here
- The darkness over Duskholm is the result of a completed ritual step
- Something in the earth recognised them at the Rootwell
- They were brought here deliberately

## Act Two — What Is True

*The player pulls on every thread and finds that each one leads somewhere uncomfortable.*

### Key Beats

**6. Nessa**
The player finds evidence of someone in the Abandoned Quarter before they find Nessa herself. When they meet, she is wary. Over time she becomes the player's most important source of Church intelligence.

She begins deciphering the Covenant markings. She recognises the ritual architecture. She is the first person to tell the player plainly what the Church has been doing — siphoning the entity's power for seven centuries — and that Serafin's knowledge of this is real and accurate, even if his response to it is catastrophic.

She has not yet learned that Serafin is in Duskholm. When she does, it needs to be the player who tells her.

**7. Rowan**
Rowan makes contact once the player's investigation reaches Covenant-adjacent territory. He does not fully defect — he asks the player to meet him, gives them specific information about one ritual site, and retreats. He is testing whether they are trustworthy before he goes further.

Over subsequent interactions he provides a map of all three convergence points, the ritual's full timeline, and his own doubts about what the completion of the ritual will actually produce. He has seen the deep consecration process. He is not sure the entity is what Serafin believes it to be.

**8. The Church Secret**
Through some combination of Nessa's knowledge, Strell's partial admissions, and physical evidence the player finds in the chapel (the mosaic, the bell tower mechanisms, documents in Strell's locked trunks), the player pieces together the full truth of the Church.

Strell, when cornered with specific evidence, does not deny it. He makes the case for the lie: seven hundred years of relative stability, smaller wars, functioning communities. He asks the player what they think will happen if the truth is known, if the siphon fails, if the entity wakes without guidance. He is not wrong that these are real questions.

The player now knows both sides are wrong in ways that matter. Serafin knows the truth and is prepared to burn everything down for it. The Church knows the truth and has built a civilisation on concealing it. Neither has asked the entity what it wants.

**9. The Saltmere Vein**
Edvin asks the player to find Brin. The cave quest runs as a mid-act side thread. Its significance to the main quest is the ley line discovery — the player physically experiences, in the cave's glow, the same quality of presence they felt at the Rootwell. The entity is not confined to the Mirewald. It runs beneath everything.

Brin's recovery and the cave notebook give Edvin enough to finally articulate what the dimming is: the entity's power, pulled thin by the siphon's destabilisation, is no longer providing the unconscious background stability that Duskholm (and everywhere else) has relied on for centuries. People are not getting sick. They are experiencing the absence of something they never knew was there.

**10. The Mirewald Push**
The player pushes into the mid-Mirewald for the first time. Tier 2 creatures. The corruption is visible and worsening. Covenant camps are present — evidence of active ritual preparation for the third consecration.

Rowan's maps allow navigation. Nessa can identify specific sites from descriptions.

At the edge of the deep forest the player finds a Penitent — a former Covenant member who underwent full consecration. They are not mindlessly hostile. They are trying to say something they no longer have the language for. This is a story moment, not a fight. What they manage to communicate, in fragments, is the first direct suggestion that the entity is aware of the player specifically.

**11. Second Meeting with Serafin**
Serafin comes to the player again. He has watched them move through both the Church's secrets and the Covenant's territory and he respects what they have done. He tells them the ritual's completion date — soon, within days. He offers them the choice plainly:

- Stand aside and let the ritual complete. The world changes. He does not know exactly how.
- Help the Church reseal the entity. The lie continues. The siphon resumes. The next waking is still coming, in another century.
- Find a third answer, if one exists.

He does not threaten. He genuinely wants the player to choose freely. He believes, deeply, that a coerced outcome serves nothing.

This is the end of Act Two. The player has all the information they are going to have. Now they must choose.

## Act Three — What Comes Next

*Everything converges at the Rootbed.*

### Key Beats

**12. The Decision**
The player's accumulated choices — which NPCs they have befriended, which factions they have cooperated with, which information they have gathered — determine which endings are available.

All three endings require the player to go to the Rootbed. The path through the Mirewald is now Tier 3 territory. The bell towers, recalibrated or broken depending on player choices, affect the difficulty of the approach.

**13. NPC Resolutions**
Before the final push, several NPC arcs close:

- **Rowan** makes his choice — faces Serafin directly or cannot. Either outcome is valid. If the romance arc is complete, this scene includes the player.
- **Nessa** confronts the reality of Serafin in Duskholm — a scene with the player that resolves her relationship to what he taught her and what he did.
- **Osric** mobilises the town guard if trust is established — they cannot follow the player into the deep Mirewald but they can hold Duskholm's walls during the final night.
- **Strell** makes a final bid for the player's cooperation and then, depending on player choices, either assists or stands aside.
- **Corren, Cael, Brin** — each has a small closing scene that reflects their arc. Not dramatic. Just present.

**14. The Rootbed**
The player reaches the Rootbed. The entity's presence is unmistakable here — the recognition felt faintly at the Rootwell is now full and specific. It does not speak. But the player understands, in a way that bypasses language, that it has been aware of them since they arrived in Duskholm. Possibly before.

Serafin is already here. He is not hostile. He is completing preparations. He is surprised the player made it this far and he shows it — the first crack in his composure, not anger but something closer to being moved.

The final confrontation is a conversation before it is a fight — and depending on the chosen ending, it may not become a fight at all.

## Endings

### Ending One — The Seal
*"The lie that works."*

The player sides with Strell. Using the Church's knowledge of the siphon architecture, the player and Strell perform a counter-ritual that reseals the entity before the third consecration can complete. Serafin is stopped — killed, imprisoned, or driven off depending on player choices.

The darkness lifts. The gates open. The Mirewald goes quiet. Duskholm survives.

The Church's secret remains intact. The siphon resumes. The entity goes back to sleep, drained further, and will wake again in some future century for someone else to deal with.

**Epilogue tone:** Relief with an undercurrent of wrongness. The player knows what they preserved. Most of Duskholm does not, and never will. Strell thanks them without quite meeting their eyes.

---

### Ending Two — The Setting
*"The truth that costs everything."*

The player stands aside and lets the ritual complete. Serafin consecrates the Rootbed. The Hollow Sun fully sets.

The entity wakes.

It is not a monster. It is not a god in any Church sense. It is vast and old and genuinely, profoundly indifferent to human structures — political, religious, architectural. The Church's power dissolves overnight. The siphon breaks. Every miracle performed in the Church's name stops. The institution collapses within a generation.

What replaces it is unknown. The entity does not offer guidance or comfort. It simply is, now awake, now present in the land in ways that are felt rather than seen. The Mirewald changes — the corruption recedes, replaced by something older and stranger. Not safe. Just true.

Serafin gets what he worked for. He does not look as certain as he expected to feel.

**Epilogue tone:** Vast and unresolved. The world is genuinely different. Whether it is better is a question the game does not answer.

---

### Ending Three — The Third Dawn
*"Neither the setting nor the seal."*

Only available if the player has: befriended Nessa and accessed her Church knowledge, fully trusted Rowan and received the complete Covenant ritual texts, reached the Rootbed and received the entity's full recognition, and found the historical record of the original pre-Church rite buried in Edvin's botanical texts.

The player performs a third ritual — not the Church's sealing, not the Covenant's waking, but the original rite that predates both. It wakes the entity without binding it to any purpose. It simultaneously exposes the Church's foundational lie by breaking the siphon in a way that cannot be concealed or reversed.

The entity wakes clean. It recognises the player specifically. What passes between them in the Rootbed is not shown — it is implied, and each player will understand it differently.

The Church falls. The Covenant, without its purpose, disbands — Serafin, when he understands what happened, does not resist. He sits down at the edge of the Rootbed and for the first time in the game he does not know what to say. The darkness lifts.

What comes after is genuinely open. Not optimistic, not pessimistic. Just unwritten.

Rowan, if his arc is complete, is standing beside the player when it happens.

**Epilogue tone:** Quiet and earned. The hardest ending to reach and the only one that feels like an actual answer rather than a choice between bad options.
