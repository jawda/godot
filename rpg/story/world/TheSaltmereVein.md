# The Saltmere Vein

## Overview

Half a mile east of Duskholm, where the land rises into a low rocky escarpment before flattening into farmland, there is a cave system the locals call the Saltmere Vein. It has been used intermittently for generations — miners pulled iron ore from it two centuries ago, herbalists collected the cave fungi and mineral deposits that grow in its deeper sections, and children dared each other to go in and come back before the light ran out.

It has never been considered dangerous. Dark, cold, and unpleasant — but not dangerous.

That has changed.

---

## Why Edvin Needs It

The Saltmere Vein grows several materials that exist nowhere else near Duskholm:

- **Veinmoss** — a pale grey-green moss that grows on the cave walls in the mid-sections. When prepared correctly it is a powerful anti-inflammatory and mild analgesic. Edvin has used it for years.
- **Saltbloom Crystals** — small mineral formations in the deeper sections, soluble in water, with properties that stabilise certain compounds and extend their shelf life. Essential for making healing tinctures last more than a few days.
- **Deepcap Fungi** — a bioluminescent mushroom that grows only in complete darkness. In small doses it treats the "dimming" symptoms Edvin has been observing in townsfolk — he does not fully understand why yet, but it works. In large doses it is hallucinogenic and potentially dangerous.

Since the darkness began, Edvin's existing stocks have been depleting faster than expected — more people are sick, more people are injured, and supply from the south has stopped. He needs someone to go into the Vein and retrieve what he cannot.

He has not gone himself. He will not explain why in early conversations. The reason: the last person he sent — a young woman named Brin, three weeks ago — did not come back.

---

## What's Inside

### The Entrance Chamber
Wide and low-ceilinged, with the remnants of old mining equipment rusting against the walls. Torch brackets still in place. The ground is wet from an underground spring. Safe, unremarkable, used as shelter by animals in bad weather.

Evidence near the entrance that someone has been here recently — a bedroll, extinguished candle stubs, a broken jar. Brin's, probably.

### The Mid-Sections
Where the Veinmoss grows and the tunnels narrow. The cave system branches here — three passages, only two of which are navigable. The third collapsed decades ago.

The corruption from the Mirewald does not reach this far directly — the Saltmere Vein is far enough east and geologically separate. However, the ley line that runs beneath Duskholm passes through the cave system. As the entity stirs, the ley line has become active in ways it wasn't before. Certain sections of rock glow faintly. The Deepcap fungi have grown larger and more numerous than Edvin has ever seen.

Something has moved in. Not a Mirewald creature — something that was already here, dormant, that the ley line activity has woken. **Cave Grippers** — pale, blind, multi-limbed arthropods the size of a large dog, normally docile scavengers that retreat from light and noise. They are no longer retreating.

### The Deep Sections
Where the Saltbloom Crystals form and the Deepcap fungi cluster thickest. The bioluminescence here is significant enough to navigate by — a cold blue-green light that makes the cave beautiful in an unsettling way.

This is where Brin went. This is where her trail ends.

The deep sections connect, via a narrow submerged passage, to an underground water system that runs beneath the Mirewald. This passage is currently blocked by a recent rockfall — something caused it from the other side. The player will not be able to go through it on their first visit. In the mid-game, after events in the Mirewald, the passage opens.

### What Happened to Brin
She is in the deep section. She is alive. She has been in the bioluminescent dark long enough that her eyes have adjusted and she does not want to leave — the Deepcap fungi release spores in large quantities and prolonged exposure has put her in a docile, dissociated state. She is not aggressive. She is sitting very still in the blue-green light and she seems content.

Getting her out without harm is a puzzle, not a fight. Edvin will know what to do once the player brings her back — he has seen something like this before, in a different context, which opens a conversation about what the cave fungi actually are and why they help with the dimming.

## Creatures

| Creature | Location | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Cave Grippers | Mid-sections | Blind, light-averse, now aggressive. Retreat from bright light sources — a torch held steady keeps them back, but they close in if the light wavers |
| Deep Lurker | Deep section (one) | A single larger Cave Gripper that has fed well. Territorial around Brin's location. Not a boss fight — avoidable with patience |
| Saltmere Bats | Throughout | Non-hostile, but disturbing them in large numbers creates noise that attracts Cave Grippers |

---

## Lore Significance

The ley line activity in the Saltmere Vein is an early indicator of something the player will understand better later: the entity's influence is not limited to the Mirewald. The ley lines run everywhere, and as it stirs, everything connected to them is affected. The cave is a contained, manageable version of what is happening on a larger scale across the region.

The submerged passage to the Mirewald's underground water system is significant in the late game — it provides an alternative entry point to the Mirewald's heart that bypasses the heavily corrupted surface zones. Getting through it requires preparation and a specific item Edvin can prepare from the Saltbloom Crystals.
