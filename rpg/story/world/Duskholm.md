# Duskholm

## Overview

Duskholm is a walled market town of roughly four hundred souls, built at the southern edge of the Mirewald where the forest thins enough to farm. It was never a wealthy place — too remote, too close to the trees — but it was stable. Wool, timber, root vegetables, a twice-yearly trade fair. Enough to live on.

Thirty days ago the sun stopped rising over it. Now the population is closer to three hundred and falling. People are not dying — they are leaving through gaps in the walls at night, abandoning everything, and not coming back. The ones who stay are either too stubborn, too frightened to move, or have reasons for remaining that they do not share.

The town smells of tallow candles and old smoke. Every window glows at all hours. No one lets their light go out.

## Districts

### The Abandoned Quarter
*Northern Duskholm — closest to the Mirewald*

The northernmost streets have been empty for two weeks. The families who lived here fled south first, and what they left behind tells the story — doors left open, meals half-eaten on tables, a child's toy in the middle of the road. The buildings are intact. Nothing burned. They just left.

The corruption from the Mirewald is strongest here. At night, Tier 1 creatures occasionally cross into these streets. The town guard has stopped patrolling north of the Rootwell.

There is something else here too: evidence, if the player looks carefully, of the Covenant. Chalk marks on certain doorframes. Cellar doors that have been padlocked from the outside. A house whose basement connects to a tunnel.

The quarter is not entirely empty. One person stayed.

**Nessa** has been living in an abandoned tanner's house on the north-east edge of the quarter for three weeks — before the evacuation finished, before the darkness fully settled. She was already here before most people knew to leave.

She is in her early twenties, small and sharp-featured, with the kind of stillness that comes from spending a lot of time being very careful not to be noticed. She wears mismatched layered clothing clearly salvaged from the empty houses around her. She knows the abandoned quarter better than any living person in Duskholm — every unlocked door, every useful cellar, every route that avoids the areas where the Covenant has been active at night.

She was a novice in the Church's Order of the Sealed Eye for four years. She did not know that was what she was. She thought she was being trained as a scholar-cleric — research, archival work, the preservation of sensitive texts. It took her a long time to understand that the texts she was helping to suppress were true, and that the "sensitive preservation" was destruction. When she understood, she took what she could carry and ran.

She has been running for eight months. She ended up in Duskholm because she was following a thread from a document she memorised before it was burned — a reference to something beneath the Mirewald. She is trying to understand what the Church has been hiding. She does not yet know that Serafin Voss is in this town, and when she finds out, her reaction will be complicated — he was her instructor for two years and she does not know whether to trust anything he ever taught her.

She will not trust the player easily. She will not trust anyone easily. But she is alone in a dark town with creatures moving through the streets at night, and she is pragmatic enough to know that alone has limits.

**Romance:** Nessa is a romance option. Her arc is the slowest of the four — she has been burned badly enough that she tests people rather than opening to them, sometimes without being fully conscious that she's doing it. The player earns her not through grand gestures or consistent presence but through small moments of honesty. The first time she chooses to trust the player with something real is a significant scene. She will not say she has feelings for the player. She will just stop hiding things from them, and that is her version of it.

**Gameplay:** Exploration zone, light stealth/combat. Key Covenant evidence found here. First Ashwalker encounter in late game. Nessa is a romance NPC and major quest ally — her knowledge of Church operations, suppressed texts, and the Sealed Eye makes her one of the most informationally valuable characters in the game.

### Rootwell Square
*Town centre*

Named for the ancient stone well at its heart — carved from a single piece of black rock that no one in Duskholm's history has been able to date. The well is deep. If you drop a stone in, you don't hear it land.

The square is where the remaining townsfolk gather during the day, mostly out of mutual fear rather than community spirit. A few market stalls still operate. People talk in low voices. Everyone watches the northern skyline.

The well is significant: it sits on one of the ley lines that feeds into the Mirewald. When the player interacts with it in the mid-game, after learning enough about the entity, they will feel the same recognition they eventually feel at the Rootbed — faint here, but unmistakable.

**Gameplay:** Central hub. NPCs congregate here. Key dialogue and quest pickups. The well is an interactable object with mid-game story relevance.

### The Chapel of the Ascending Dawn
*Centre-north, facing south — deliberately turned away from the Mirewald*

The largest building in Duskholm, built from pale stone that looks almost white against the perpetual grey sky. Its bell tower is the tallest structure in town. The doors are never locked — Canon Strell has made sure of that, wanting to project calm.

Inside: long pews, tallow candles in iron holders, a large mosaic of the sun rising over a mountain on the rear wall. The mosaic is old — old enough that if the player examines it carefully, they will notice the sun in the image is not fully above the horizon. It never rises all the way. The artist was trying to say something.

Strell has set up his operation in the vestry behind the altar. His locked trunks are there. His acolytes sleep in the side rooms. He conducts "private services" in the early hours that no parishioner has ever attended.

**Gameplay:** Key NPC location (Canon Strell). The bell tower is a gameplay mechanic — the player can interact with the tuning apparatus in the tower to stabilize or destabilize local spawn rates. Late-game the chapel becomes a potential refuge or a target.

### The Tallow Inn
*South-east, near the main gate*

Where the player wakes up. Three storeys of old timber, perpetually warm inside from a fire that the innkeeper, **Maret**, has not let go out since the darkness began. The common room is crowded at all hours — people come here for the light as much as the ale.

The food is simple and slightly worse than it should be (supply lines from the south have thinned). The rooms upstairs are small. The player's room is on the third floor, east-facing. The note was on the floor inside a locked door.

Maret is sharp, observant, and one of the few people in town who will speak plainly to the player. She has lived in Duskholm her whole life. She knows something is wrong beyond just the darkness, but she doesn't know what. She will be one of the player's best sources of local information — not because she has answers, but because she has been watching people for thirty years.

There is a man who has been nursing the same drink in the corner of the common room for four days. Maret has noticed. She has not asked him to leave.

**Rowan** came to Duskholm with the Covenant five weeks ago. He is thirty-two, broad-shouldered, with a quiet face and the kind of careful composure that takes effort to maintain. He joined the Covenant three years ago — genuinely, willingly, after watching the Church destroy his home village over a land dispute dressed up as a heresy charge. Serafin found him at the right moment, offered him truth and purpose, and Rowan accepted both with open hands.

He still believes the Church is corrupt. He still believes the truth about the ancient power deserves to be known. What he did not fully understand until he saw the ritual sites in the Mirewald — until he saw what the Covenant was actually doing to the creatures there, what was being done to the people who volunteered for deeper consecration — was that believing the right things is not sufficient cover for any action taken in their name.

He has not reported his doubts to Serafin. He has not left. He is sitting in the corner of the Tallow Inn trying to work out the difference between cowardice and conscience, and he has been at it for four days.

He has not spoken to the player yet. He will make the first move — a quiet word, a slip of paper pushed across a table — when the player's investigation brings them close enough to Covenant activity that Rowan decides they are the best available option for what he is considering.

He is not a traitor to the Covenant's ideals. He is a traitor to its methods. There is a distinction and it matters to him deeply and the player will need to decide whether it matters to them.

**Romance:** Rowan is a romance option. He is not charming in any conventional way — he doesn't flirt, doesn't deflect with humor, doesn't perform warmth. He is simply honest, almost uncomfortably so, in a way that becomes its own kind of intimacy. His romance arc runs alongside his moral arc — the player cannot fully have one without engaging with the other. If the player helps him find a path through his crisis of conscience, he gives them his loyalty in the particular way that people do when someone has seen them at their worst and stayed.

**Gameplay:** Player home base. Rest/save point. Maret as recurring NPC and information source. Notice board for side quests. Rowan is a romance NPC and a double-edged quest ally — his Covenant knowledge is invaluable but trusting him carries risk, and other characters (Nessa especially) will have strong opinions about it.

### The Apothecary
*South-west, near market*

Run by **Brother Edvin**, an elderly man who was a Church healer for thirty years before retiring to Duskholm. He is not part of the Order of the Sealed Eye. He simply knows medicine and herbs and wanted a quiet end to his life.

He is not getting one.

Edvin has been treating townsfolk for symptoms he has never seen in any medical text — not illness exactly, more like a slow dimming. People losing their appetite, their energy, their ability to feel cold or heat. He suspects the darkness is doing something to them. He is closer to the truth than he knows.

He sells healing items and can identify strange materials the player brings back from the Mirewald. He is also sitting on a collection of old botanical texts that describe certain Mirewald plants in terms that imply the forest was once used for medicine — before the Church declared it off-limits.

His assistant, **Brin**, has been missing for three weeks. He sent her to the Saltmere Vein to collect supplies and she did not return. He has told no one officially — partly out of guilt, partly because he suspects what happened and does not want to say it aloud. Her name is on Warden Osric's missing persons list. Edvin cannot bring himself to look at the shelf where her notes are still stacked.

**Gameplay:** Shop — consumables, healing, crafting materials. Quest NPC for investigation threads. The botanical texts are an optional lore item. Triggers the Saltmere Vein quest.

### The Smithy
*South-west*

**Corren**, a woman in her mid-twenties who inherited the smithy from her father and has no intention of leaving it regardless of what comes out of the forest. Lean and wiry with the kind of functional muscle that comes from years at the forge rather than any effort to have it — her forearms are as strong as most men twice her size and she knows it. She keeps her hair tied back, always has a smudge of soot somewhere on her face, and has the posture of someone who has never once been intimidated.

She is blunt, practical, and the most openly sceptical person in town about the Church's reassurances. She has been quietly reinforcing window shutters and door frames for townsfolk who can't pay her. She has also been making things she doesn't talk about — short blades, mostly. Just in case.

She does not open up easily. She respects competence and directness and has no patience for people who talk around things. If the player earns her trust she is fiercely loyal — and the only person in Duskholm who will tell them hard truths without softening them.

**Romance:** Corren is a romance option. She does not respond to flattery or grand gestures. She responds to the player showing up, doing difficult things, and not making a fuss about it. Her romance arc is slow and understated — a shared task, a quiet moment at the forge, the gradual erosion of her carefully maintained self-sufficiency.

**Gameplay:** Weapons and armour shop. Upgrade/repair service. Quest ally. Romance NPC.

### The Glasshouse
*South-centre, between the smithy and the mill*

The only place in Duskholm that does not feel like Duskholm.

**Sera**, a retired glassblower in her sixties, built the Glasshouse thirty years ago as her workshop and has since converted it into something between a sanctuary and a stubborn act of defiance. The structure is mostly glass panels in iron frames, filled with hanging lanterns and living plants — herbs, climbing vines, small flowering things that have no business surviving without sunlight and are surviving anyway because Sera refuses to let them die. The colored glass panels scatter the lanternlight into fractured warm pools of amber, rose, and gold across the floor.

She charges nothing for entry. She asks only that you leave your problems at the door — not forever, just while you're inside. Most people find this surprisingly easy.

The Glasshouse smells of warm earth, rosemary, and beeswax. There is always something brewing on a small iron stove in the corner. Sera will offer the player tea without being asked and will talk about anything except the darkness, which she considers a rude topic indoors.

It is not naive optimism. Sera has lived long enough to know that things go wrong and keep going wrong and you maintain the small beautiful things anyway, precisely because they are not guaranteed. The Glasshouse is her argument against despair — not a refutation of it, just a counter-offer.

**Cael**, a young cartographer from the capital, has taken up semi-permanent residence at a corner table here. He arrived in Duskholm six weeks ago to map the Mirewald region for a university commission and has been stuck ever since. He is making the best of it in the most Cael way possible: by cataloguing everything he observes with meticulous, slightly obsessive detail in a series of notebooks that are rapidly running out of pages.

He is in his mid-twenties, slight and dark-haired, with ink-stained fingers and the look of someone who is permanently thinking about three things at once. He is warm, genuinely curious, and quick to find something interesting in whatever the player tells him. He is also quietly terrified and doing an extremely good job of not showing it — his notebooks are how he keeps the fear at bay, the same way Sera keeps her plants alive.

He has been mapping more than geography. He has notes on the bell towers, on the creature sightings, on the patterns of which buildings in the Abandoned Quarter were marked and which weren't. He doesn't know what any of it means yet. The player might.

**Romance:** Cael is a romance option. He falls a little in love with people who tell him things he doesn't know yet — which means he falls a little in love with the player almost immediately and spends the rest of the game trying to work out if that's real or just intellectual excitement. His romance arc is warm and slightly fumbling, full of him saying the wrong thing at the right moment and the right thing at the wrong moment.

**Gameplay:** Glasshouse is the rest/warmth location — a tonal break from the gothic atmosphere. Sera is a minor NPC and ambient character. Cael is a romance NPC and significant quest ally — his maps and observations are a major source of investigative leads. His notebooks can be read as optional lore items.

### The Old Warden's Hall
*East side, now largely disused*

The town guard operates out of here, though "operates" is generous. The captain, **Warden Osric**, is a competent man suffering from the particular paralysis of someone whose authority has stopped meaning anything. He has twelve guards. Two have deserted. The others follow his orders but not his lead — they take direction but they don't trust that anyone knows what to do.

Osric will be suspicious of the player initially. He has watched too many strangers arrive in Duskholm in the past month and cause problems he then has to manage. He is not corrupt, not involved with the Covenant or the Church's inner workings. He just wants his town back.

He can become one of the player's most valuable late-game allies if trust is established — he knows Duskholm's layout better than anyone and he can mobilize the guard.

**Gameplay:** Quest NPC. Guard captain trust-building arc. Unlocks guard support in late-game events.

### The Charnel House
*North-west, near the Abandoned Quarter*

Where Duskholm's dead are prepared for burial. The gravedigger, **Pell**, is a thin young man who has been unusually busy lately and handles it with unsettling cheerfulness. He is one of the few people who regularly goes near the Abandoned Quarter — the overflow graves from the main churchyard are up there.

He has noticed things. Graves that have been disturbed — not dug up from outside, but pushed out from within, like something tried to leave. He has not told anyone. He is not sure anyone would believe him.

**Gameplay:** Quest trigger location. The disturbed graves are an early hint at what the Covenant is doing. Pell is an optional NPC with significant lore if the player befriends him.

---

### The Bell Towers
*Four towers, one at each compass point of the town walls*

Built by the Church two centuries ago, officially described as alarm towers. In practice they are the physical anchors of the siphon architecture — each tower contains a brass tuning mechanism in its base, calibrated to a specific harmonic that channels the entity's energy away from the town and toward the Church's collection points.

When working correctly, they suppress Mirewald creature activity at Duskholm's borders. As the siphon destabilizes, their calibration drifts. The player will be able to observe this — Tier 1 creatures appearing closer to town correlates directly with bell tower malfunction.

Each tower's mechanism can be interacted with. The player can attempt to recalibrate them (reducing nearby spawns temporarily), or they can choose to fully disable them (accelerating the destabilization for reasons that will make sense in specific quest paths).

Strell knows what the towers really are. He will not say so directly.

**Gameplay:** Interactive mechanic tied to world state. Recalibrating = temporary spawn suppression. Disabling = escalation. Four towers = four choices the player makes over the course of the game.

### The South Gate
*Sealed*

The main entrance to Duskholm. Two heavy iron-banded wooden gates, closed and barred from the inside thirty days ago when the darkness began. Officially this was Warden Osric's order — containment, protection, keep people in and things out.

The gates do not open. Not because of the bar — the bar has been removed and replaced twice — but because something is wrong with the hinges. The wood has swollen. The iron has fused in a way that Corren examined and could not explain. It is as if the gate decided to stay shut.

There is a smaller postern door to the east of the gate that can be forced. Most of the people who have left Duskholm used it. It leads to the southern road, which leads to other towns, which reportedly have sunlight. The player could use it.

They just can't make themselves go through it. Something keeps them here.

**Gameplay:** Narrative device reinforcing the trapped feeling. The postern door is technically accessible but the player's personal hook prevents leaving. Late-game the main gates become significant.

## Atmosphere Notes

- **No natural light.** Every scene is candlelit, lantern-lit, or firelit. The sky is a permanent deep grey. Stars are occasionally visible but the moon never fully appears — just a diffuse brightening behind clouds.
- **Sound design cues:** distant animal sounds from the Mirewald that cut off abruptly. The irregular tone of malfunctioning bell towers. The constant low creak of the Tallow Inn's fire.
- **The people of Duskholm** are not panicking. They passed panic three weeks ago. They are in the numb, functional state that comes after — going through routines, keeping lights burning, not looking north.
- **Weather:** permanent overcast, occasional cold rain, never snow. The temperature has dropped since the darkness began.
