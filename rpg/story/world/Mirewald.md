# The Mirewald

## Overview

The Mirewald is the ancient forest that encircles Duskholm on three sides. It has always been considered ill-omened — locals do not hunt in it, children are told to stay out of it, and the Church built Duskholm's chapel facing deliberately away from its treeline. Old people call it the Breathwood. They do not explain why.

It is old in a way that most forests are not. The trees are black-barked and vast, their canopies so dense that the forest floor never fully dries. The ground is soft and dark, riddled with root systems that run deeper than they should. Standing at the treeline, you can sometimes hear something that isn't wind.

For most of Duskholm's history the Mirewald was simply unpleasant. Something to avoid.

That changed thirty days ago.

---

## The Corruption

When the ancient entity began to stir — its long sleep disturbed by the combination of the Covenant's ritual work and centuries of the Church's siphoning reaching a critical threshold — the energy it radiates stopped flowing quietly through the ley lines and began *bleeding* outward.

The Mirewald sits directly above the entity's resting place. It absorbs the overflow first.

The corruption does not work like a poison. It works like a signal — a vast, unconscious broadcast that the entity emits as it dreams closer to waking. Creatures exposed to it do not die. They *change*. Their behaviour shifts first: animals become territorial and then aggressive and then something worse than aggressive. Their bodies follow. The changes are not random — they trend toward older, stranger forms, as if the signal is rewriting them according to a blueprint that predates their species.

The longer the exposure, the deeper the change. The closer to the heart of the Mirewald, the stronger the signal.

---

## Creature Tiers

The corruption radiates outward in rough bands from the forest's heart. Enemy difficulty and strangeness scales with proximity to the centre.

### Tier 1 — The Edge (Outer Mirewald / Duskholm borders)
*Early game. The corruption is fresh and shallow here.*

Creatures that have only recently been touched. Behavioural changes are obvious but physical changes are subtle — wrong eyes, unnatural stillness, movement that's slightly off.

- **Hollow Wolves** — Pack hunters with blackened fur and eyes that reflect light like mirrors. They do not howl. They coordinate in silence.
- **Ravel Crows** — Ravens moving in dense, geometric formations. They do not scatter. They watch. Occasionally they dive in coordinated strikes.
- **Mireswine** — Feral boars with fungal growths along their spines. Aggressive, unpredictable, unnaturally fast.
- **Creeping Rot** — Patches of animated root and soil that have absorbed enough overflow to move. Slow, but they don't stop.

### Tier 2 — The Deepwood (Mid Mirewald)
*Mid game. Physical corruption is significant. These were recognisable things that no longer are.*

- **The Turned** — People who wandered into the Mirewald and did not come back. Not undead — still alive, still breathing, still occasionally attempting speech. What they say is not language anymore. They move on all fours.
- **Barrow Stags** — Deer that have grown secondary bone structures from their skulls and shoulders, like armour growing from the inside out. They charge without provocation.
- **Wychwood Shades** — Thin, tall figures made entirely of compressed shadow and root matter. They appear only where no light reaches. They do not have faces but they orient toward sound.
- **Drowned Wardens** — Ancient guardian spirits of the forest, originally benign, now fully inverted by the overflow. They look like tall humanoids wrapped in wet bark. They were once the reason the Mirewald was merely unpleasant rather than lethal.

### Tier 3 — The Heart (Deep Mirewald / Ritual Sites)
*Late game. The signal here is strong enough to physically alter the environment. Trees move. The ground breathes.*

- **Root Colossi** — Enormous constructs of compressed root and stone animated by direct overflow from the entity. They are not aware of the player specifically — they are more like an immune response. They attack anything not marked by the Covenant.
- **The Penitent** — Former Covenant members who volunteered for full immersion in the heart's energy as part of early ritual work. They are no longer human but retain fragments of purpose. They protect the ritual sites.
- **Ashwalkers** — Creatures made from the residue of sites the Covenant consecrated — including, potentially, something from Ashveil. Humanoid silhouettes made of compacted ash that hold their shape loosely, shedding and reforming as they move.

---

## Escalation — The Corruption Spreads

As the Covenant's ritual progresses, the corruption expands outward. Tier 1 creatures begin appearing at Duskholm's edge. Tier 2 creatures push into the outer Mirewald. The pressure builds.

This should be felt by the player as urgency:
- NPCs report livestock disappearing closer to town
- The treeline visibly darkens as the ritual advances
- Creatures that were previously only in the mid-forest begin appearing on roads

If the ritual is not stopped, Tier 1 creatures begin entering Duskholm itself.

---

## Gameplay Notes

- The Mirewald should function as the primary combat zone, with the player pushing deeper as they progress the main quest
- Creature spawns in the outer ring should respawn on a cycle — the corruption is ongoing, not a one-time clear
- Key story locations (ritual sites, the entity's resting place, Covenant camps) are gated behind the deeper tiers
- The Church's bell towers in Duskholm were originally built to suppress creature activity at the forest edge — when they begin malfunctioning (as the siphon destabilizes), Tier 1 spawns should increase noticeably near town
- The Ashwalkers are a late reveal — the player should recognise what they are made of

## The Heart

At the absolute centre of the Mirewald is a place the old maps call the Rootbed. It is a depression in the earth roughly forty metres across where no trees grow. The soil is black and fine, like ash, and it is warm to the touch. The root systems of every tree in the Mirewald converge here, running beneath the surface like veins.

This is where the entity rests. This is where the ritual must be completed — or stopped.

Standing at the Rootbed, even for a few seconds, the player should experience something: not a vision, not a voice, but a feeling of being *recognised*. The entity does not communicate in language. But something in the player's chest responds to it, like a key turning in a lock they didn't know they had.

What that means is for the late game to answer.
