# Opening Sequence

## Overview

The opening sequence serves three purposes simultaneously: it introduces the world and its immediate danger, it handles all character creation in a way that feels embedded in the fiction, and it establishes Maret as the player's first trusted contact before they have spoken to anyone else.

Character creation is never presented as a menu. Every choice is framed as something happening in the world.

---

## Structure at a Glance

1. **Arrival** — short guided walk, player reaches the Tallow Inn
2. **Check-in** — Maret asks the player's name *(name input)*
3. **The Room** — player unpacks, chooses their gear *(class selection)*
4. **Sleep**
5. **The Waking** — Maret knocks, something is wrong outside
6. **Background Moment** — how the player was before the knock *(background selection)*
7. **Gearing Up** — player equips themselves, exits room
8. **Downstairs** — Maret gives the situation
9. **Opening Event** — creatures in the street, player acts

---

## Scene Script

### Beat 1 — Arrival

*Exterior. Night. The road into Duskholm from the south.*

*The player is walking. No dialogue, no narration. Just movement — the road, the walls ahead, the gate standing open. The guards on the gate look tired and do not speak. One of them watches the player pass without expression.*

*Inside the walls: candlelight from every window. The smell of woodsmoke. The streets quieter than a town this size should be at this hour. A dog somewhere, not barking — just watching.*

*The Tallow Inn is visible at the end of the street. Warm light through the shuttered windows. The player walks toward it.*

*This segment is short — thirty seconds of movement, no interaction required. Long enough for the world to register. Short enough not to overstay.*

---

### Beat 2 — Check-in with Maret

*Interior. The Tallow Inn common room. A fire burning. Several townsfolk at tables, not talking much. Maret behind the bar, already looking at the door when the player enters — she looks at every door when it opens.*

**MARET:**
"Traveller. You look like you've been walking since yesterday."

*She pulls out a ledger without waiting for an answer.*

**MARET:**
"I've got a room on the third floor. East-facing. Quieter than the street side, which matters more than it used to."

*She sets the ledger on the bar and turns it toward the player. A quill.*

**MARET:**
"Name, for the record."

---

> **[PLAYER INPUT — CHARACTER NAME]**
> *The player types their character's name. Maret writes it in the ledger.*

---

*Maret looks at the name, then at the player. A beat.*

**MARET:**
"Don't recognise the name. Passing through?"

*She does not wait for a full answer — she's already reading something in the player's face and gear.*

**MARET:**
"You're not a merchant. And you're not running from something, or you'd have that look. The ones running always have that look."

*She slides a key across the bar.*

**MARET:**
"Third floor, end of the hall. There's water in the pitcher, candle by the bed. Don't let it go out. People here don't let their lights go out."

*She says this last part without particular drama. It is simply a fact about living in Duskholm right now.*

---

### Beat 3 — The Room / Class Selection

*The player's room. Small. Low ceiling. The window looks east over rooftops toward the dark mass of the Mirewald treeline, just visible in the far distance.*

*The player's traveling gear is laid out on the chair by the bed — the things they carry, the things that define what they are. As the player moves to inspect their gear, a prompt appears.*

---

> **[CLASS SELECTION — "What do you carry?"]**

*Presented as the player looking at their own things. No stat sheets, no menus — just a description of the gear and a choice.*

---

**WARRIOR / KNIGHT**
*A sword, well-maintained. Leather armor with iron reinforcing at the shoulders and chest, worn enough to have seen real use. A small whetstone in the side pocket of the pack, because you always keep the edge.*
*"You've been fighting long enough that the gear stopped feeling heavy."*

**ROGUE / SHADOWBLADE**
*Two short blades, balanced for throwing as much as stabbing. Light leather — nothing that makes noise when you move. A length of dark cord. A lockpick set wrapped in cloth at the bottom of the pack, the way you always pack it, in case.*
*"You travel light because light means fast, and fast has kept you alive."*

**MAGE / ARCANIST**
*A worn journal filled with notation that isn't quite language. A focus — a carved piece of dark wood fitted to your palm, smooth from years of use. Robes that look plain until the light catches something in the fabric.*
*"The world has edges most people can't see. You learned to see them young, and you've never fully stopped."*

**CLERIC / FAITHBOUND**
*A symbol of the Ascending Dawn on a leather cord. A small book of liturgy, the margins filled with your own notes that increasingly disagree with the printed text. A mace, blessed, that you have used more than you expected to when you took your vows.*
*"Faith is the thing you carry even when the load gets heavy. Especially then."*

**RANGER / WAYFINDER**
*A shortbow and a quiver of twelve arrows, each one fletched by hand. A long knife. A set of travelling clothes built for weather and distance. A map of the region — hand-drawn, detailed, with your own marks added at the edges.*
*"You know the road between places better than you know the places themselves. That suits you."*

---

*The player chooses. Their gear appears on the chair resolved — the specific items of their class. They set it aside for morning and lie down.*

---

### Beat 4 — Sleep

*The candle burns. The player's view narrows slowly — not a cut to black, a genuine dimming, the way sleep takes you.*

*Outside, distantly, the sound of the Mirewald. Animal sounds, ordinary enough. Then — silence. The forest going quiet all at once.*

*The player is asleep before they notice.*

---

### Beat 5 — The Waking

*A knock. Hard. Not polite.*

**MARET (through door):**
"Up. Something's in the street. Now."

*The player's view snaps awake — fast, disorienting. The candle is still burning. Outside the window the sky is the deep grey of an hour before dawn.*

*Another knock.*

**MARET:**
"I know you heard me."

---

### Beat 6 — Background Moment

*The player is awake, in the moment before they move. A beat of interiority — not narration, but a small choice that defines who they were before the road.*

---

> **[BACKGROUND SELECTION — "Before the knock"]**

*Framed as a single line describing what was true a moment ago. Each option provides a small gameplay modifier in the opening event.*

---

**SURVIVOR**
*You were already awake. The silence from the forest woke you ten minutes ago and you've been watching the window since. Some part of you never really stops watching.*
*Modifier: Increased awareness — enemies in the opening event are revealed on approach.*

**SOLDIER**
*You were in the middle of a dream about marching. The knock pulled you out of it clean — no disorientation, no fumbling. You're already calculating before your feet hit the floor.*
*Modifier: Opening combat — bonus to first strike damage.*

**OUTCAST**
*You'd been lying awake anyway. New places do that to you — too many unknowns, too many doors you haven't checked. You weren't worried, exactly. Just ready.*
*Modifier: Movement speed increase during the opening event.*

**FAITHFUL**
*You'd been praying. Quietly, the way you do when a place feels wrong and you want something to push against the wrongness. The knock felt like an answer of a kind.*
*Modifier: Starting bonus to healing items — one extra potion.*

**WANDERER**
*You'd heard it before Maret knocked. The shift in the forest sounds, the quality of the silence after. You'd been pulling your boots on already.*
*Modifier: Reduced stamina cost during the opening event.*

---

### Beat 7 — Gearing Up

*The player dresses and equips. This is the first time the inventory/equipment screen is accessible — a brief, low-pressure introduction to the UI.*

*Maret's footsteps retreat down the hall. She doesn't wait.*

*The player collects their gear from the chair. The room looks the same as when they arrived, except for the note on the floor by the door — they don't see it yet. It was slipped under while they slept.*

*They leave without noticing it. They will find it when they return.*

---

### Beat 8 — Downstairs

*Common room. The fire is still going. Three or four townsfolk are standing near the door, not going through it. Maret is already at the bar pulling something from under it — a short axe she sets on the counter and looks at without picking up.*

**MARET:**
"Something came through the gap in the east wall. Osric's men are there but they're — "

*She stops. Sounds from outside — not screaming, something worse. The low sound of something moving that isn't moving right.*

**MARET:**
"There are two of them. Maybe three. Wolves, or — something that was wolves."

*She looks at the player's gear. Then at the player.*

**MARET:**
"You look like someone who's dealt with worse."

*It's half a question.*

---

### Beat 9 — Opening Event: The East Wall

*Exterior. The alley behind the east wall. Dawn still an hour away.*

*Two Hollow Wolves are in the street. They are not moving like wolves — too still between movements, too coordinated. Their eyes catch the lanternlight wrong. One of Osric's guards is backed against a wall, injured, not going anywhere. Another is trying to hold a line with a spear he clearly does not want to use.*

*The player acts.*

---

**EVENT STRUCTURE:**

This is the first combat encounter — designed to be winnable at all starting classes, short enough to serve as a tutorial, and meaningful enough to matter.

- **Two Hollow Wolves** — manageable for a solo player with basic gear
- **Injured guard** — Osric's man, can be protected for a small trust bonus with Osric later
- **Optional:** a third wolf breaks off and runs — not defeated, escaped back toward the treeline. Establishes that the threat is ongoing, not resolved.

After the combat:

*The guard Osric's man straightens up. He looks at the player — really looks.*

**GUARD:**
"...Where did you come from?"

*He doesn't wait for an answer. He's already moving to check on his colleague.*

*Maret is behind the player. She watched the whole thing.*

**MARET:**
"Come back inside. I'll put the kettle on."

*She says it the same way she said everything else — without drama. As if this is just what mornings are like now.*

---

### Beat 10 — Return to the Inn / The Note

*Back in the common room. Maret makes tea. This is the first proper conversation — a chance for the player to ask basic questions about the town, the darkness, the Mirewald. Maret answers plainly, without panic, in the style established by her character.*

*She does not mention Canon Strell. She does not mention the Covenant. She doesn't know about those things yet — or rather, she doesn't know the player is worth telling yet.*

*After the conversation, the player can return to their room.*

*The note is on the floor.*

*The player picks it up. A wax seal — a circle with a downward line. Three words:*

***"You were summoned."***

*The main quest begins.*

---

|---|---|---|
| Name | Check-in, Maret's ledger | Written into the inn register |
